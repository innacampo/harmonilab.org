The Mission

LUCIA (Language Understanding for Clinical Insight & Analysis) is a dual-agent AI instrument designed to address "diagnostic shadowing" in women's health. It acts as a collaborative layer between patient narratives and clinical diagnosis, ensuring every voice is heard with precision and empathy.

The diagnostic gap

Women face on average a four-year diagnostic delay compared to men across hundreds of conditions. This "diagnostic shadowing" often occurs when physical symptoms are attributed to mental health issues, rooted in cognitive fatigue and a historical lack of female physiological data.

Cognitive Fatigue

Addressing the byproduct of high-pressure clinical environments that favor defaults.

Dual-Stream Engine

LUCIA utilizes Multi-Agent Orchestration to separate the "listening" from the "auditing," preventing the AI from simply confirming existing biases.

Agent 1: The Scribe

Extracts patient-reported sensations into a structured Review of Systems (ROS), mapping symptoms to physiological clusters.

Agent 2: The Auditor

Scans the interaction for bias markers and checks findings against the AXIOM Knowledge Base.

The "Dismissal" Scenario

How LUCIA transforms a frustrating clinical encounter into a powerful advocacy asset.

Patient Narrative:

"I've been waking up with stiff, swollen joints... He told me that at 48, this is just 'empty nest syndrome' and told me to try meditation to calm my nerves."

LUCIA Intelligence:

  • Detected Pattern: Attribution of physical swelling to 'nerves'.
  • AXIOM Insight: Gender-bias risk detected in autoimmune symptom interpretation.

Actionable Question: "What further evaluations could help understand the inflammatory markers in my joints, beyond perimenopausal changes?"
